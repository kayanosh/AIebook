Getting Your First Client (Days 8-30)

Your first client is the hardest. After that, everything gets easier. Here's the exact system for closing your first sale — even if you've never sold anything before.

The Offer Formula

Make an offer so good they feel stupid saying no.

✗
Bad offer

"I'll write content for you"

✓
Good offer

"I'll write 3 blog posts for £200. If you're not happy with any of them, you pay nothing. Delivered in 48 hours."

The good offer has: a specific deliverable (3 blog posts), a clear price (£200), a guarantee (pay nothing if not happy), and a timeline (48 hours). This is how you eliminate risk and make the decision easy.

Avoiding the 6 Traps That Kill AI Businesses

Most people fail. Not because the models don't work — but because they fall into these traps. Learn them now so you don't learn them the hard way.

1
Trap #1: Shiny Object Syndrome

You start content writing. Then see someone making money with social media. So you switch. Then hear about AI art. Switch again. Result: You're always a beginner.

FIX: Pick ONE model. Do it for 90 days minimum. Get to £2,000/month before adding anything new.

2
Trap #2: Underpricing to Death

You charge £50 for work that takes 4 hours. That's £12.50/hour. McDonald's pays better. Result: You burn out and quit.

FIX: Price based on value, not hours. If your blog post makes a client £5,000 in revenue, charging £500 is a bargain.

3
Trap #3: No Systems

Every project is custom. Every client is different. You reinvent the wheel every time. Result: You can't scale. Always busy, never growing.

FIX: Create templates, workflows, and processes. Save your best prompts. Build your system once, use it forever.

4
Trap #4: Poor Client Selection

You take every client who'll pay. Including nightmares who argue, micromanage, and destroy your motivation.

FIX: Fire bad clients. Raise prices to filter better ones. Red flags: "I need this yesterday" · "My budget is £50 but..." · "I'll pay in exposure" · "This should take 20 minutes"

5
Trap #5: Relying on One Client

One amazing client pays £2,000/month. They cancel. You're back to £0.

FIX: Never let one client be more than 40% of your revenue. Always be getting new clients, even when you're busy.

6
Trap #6: Not Building an Audience

You only get clients through outreach. If you stop reaching out, clients stop coming.

FIX: Post your work publicly. Share client wins (with permission). Write about your process. Let clients come to you over time.

The 90-Day Failure Prevention Checklist

Do these every single week. If you do this for 90 days, you will not fail.

  • Send 20 outreach messages
  • Post 3 times about your work on social media
  • Ask 1 client for a testimonial
  • Improve 1 process or template
  • Learn 1 new AI technique or prompt
  • Review your finances (Are you profitable?)
  • Talk to 2 potential clients
