Most people fail. Here's why (and how to avoid it).

Trap #1: Shiny Object Syndrome
You start with content writing. Then you see someone making money with social media management. So you switch. Then you hear about AI art. So you switch again.

Result: You're always a beginner. You never master anything.

Solution: Pick ONE model. Do it for 90 days minimum. Get to Â£2000/month before adding anything new.

Trap #2: Underpricing to Death
You charge Â£50 for a service that takes you 4 hours. That's Â£12.50/hour. McDonalds pays better.

Solution: Price based on value, not hours. If your blog post makes a client Â£5000, charge Â£500, not Â£50.

Trap #3: No Systems
Every project is custom. Every client is different. You reinvent the wheel every time.

Result: You can't scale. You're always busy but never growing.

Solution: Create templates, workflows, and processes. Document everything. Build your system.

Trap #4: Poor Client Selection
You take every client who'll pay you. Including nightmare clients who argue, don't pay, and suck your soul.

Result: You hate your business.

Solution: Fire bad clients. Raise prices to filter better clients. Say no to red flags.

"I need this done yesterday"
"My budget is Â£50 but..."
"I'll pay you in exposure"

Trap #5: Relying on One Client
You have one amazing client paying you Â£2000/month. Then they cancel.

Trap #6: Not Building an Audience
You get clients through outreach only. If you stop outreach, clients stop coming.

Result: You're always hustling for the next client.

Solution: Build an audience. Post your work. Share client wins. Write threads. Make videos. Let clients come to you.
